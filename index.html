Amazon Associate Started Tour Redesign

Introduction

Amazon Associate has been widely used by huge amout of users to monetize their blogs and websites. The reasons publishers like it so much are mainly because of its brand image, variety of products, upselling ability and ease of use/integration. My goal in this project is to enhance users intention of using the service.

My Role

User Experience Designer and Prototyper

Design Constraint

Insufficient time and user research information.

Design Process
Identify Usability issues

There are couple main issues that I noticed in the current website. They're listed below.

  1. 1. Information Architecture
  2. 2. Inconsistent Behavior
  3. 3. Registration Process
  4. 4. Installation Process
All of the these issues need future investigation with web analytics and usability test reports for confirming and categorizing problems. Potential web analytics data are traffic, bounce rate, exit rate, conversion rate, landing/ bouncing page, usability tests results focusing on each part of the website and main task scenarios targeting different personas.

User Research

With no web analytics information handy, what I did is to Google and read through customer reviews of amazon affiliate program online.

Target problem

In the project I draw my attention on the "get started tour" in the website, due to the importance of the tutorial in terms of educating/ attracting new comers.

The original design

Problems in the current get started tours are

  1. 1. No clear system status
  2. 2. Lack of connections between elements in tutorial and in the website
  3. 3. Confusing navigation and color changing
  4. 4. Consistency issues in page structure
  5. 5. Content is not customer centric
Prototyping

With the ambition of helping our new comers with and without experience about affiliate programs to quickly understand our products, I started to redesign the "get started tour."

Along the way of redesigning the website, I put the emphasis on couple things,

  1. 1. Show system status with steps on the top and the steps also work as breadcrumb to make users can easily retrieve back.
  2. 2. At the second page, making users clearly understand what kinds of options they have clearly at one screen and direct them to the right path.
  3. 3. Considering there might be an original intention to lock users in the tutorial by removing the links in pages, after clicking into the blue buttons in the redesign version of tutorial. System will open a new tab in browsers and users are still somehow staying in the tutorial.
  4. 4. Adding step four, speed up tracks, as an attraction for new comers both with and without affiliate program experience to provide the most efficient way of embedding our links and also promote the new features.

Future Work

Like I mentioned before, all the issues discovered needs to be verified by usability test and web analytics reports. Also, by the user research work, clear uesr segments can be defined and design works/ features will be able to target the exact personas. My ambition is really to start redesgining the website and task flow from the very first interaction, dig into the site structure, design problems and also possibly create new features/ widgets to increase intention of being an amazon affiliate.

UWFoody

Introduction

This project was completed in the fall of 2011 for IMT540: Design Methods for Interactions and Systems. The goal of the project is to deliver best food option for student in UW and also get familiar with design process.

My Role

Heavily involve in all the steps, especially for prototype design and development by Axure

Design Process

Our project was based on 2013 CHI Student Design Competition design problem, "Changing Perspectives Through Collaboration". From the problem statement, " This year challenge is to design an object, interface, system, or service intended to help us to develop and share awareness, understanding or appreciation for our collective and collaborative crowd experience as it relates to our changing perspectives through collaboration."

Base on the topic, we came out with our design question.

Design question

How can students choose the best food options in and around campus?

Investigation

Base on our design and research questions, investigation/ user research is conducted by survey, interview, and guided tour(​inspired by IDEO method card). By the end of investigation stage, primary and secondary personas are built by what we learnt from the researches.

Ideation

Ideation stage starts by making lots of(30+) sketches with who our users in mind. After sketching individually and having discussion with team members, we evaluate the ideas and combine three of our sketches to come out with one finalized idea which is an iOS application as a solution to our design questions.

Prototyping

To bring the idea closer to reality, the interactive prototype is built up by Axure.

Evaluation

By the prototyping built in prototyping stage, we conduct usability test and evaluate the interaction between our app and users. Finally, redesign some of the pages in the prototype base on the result of usability test.

Documentation

Last but the least, design specification is written for clarification and as a report for our final project.

The project is conducted for class HCDE 518, User-centered Design taught by Jess Holbrook. Growler is a comprehensive personal home security system with a unique focus on deterring potential intruders before a home breach occurs.

My Role

I'm heavily involved in all the stages especially interactive prototype building.

The focus of our team project is to design a physical product.

Survey is designed and set up on Survey Gizmo for the purpose of collecting relatively large amount of data in short time to give us a sense of people's attitude towards current home security system. After analyzing 100+ response and interview with 7 participants, we build up our primary and secondary personas.

Ideate with 30+ scketches and finalize to 2 ideas and storyboards.

The usability test is conducted by our interactive prototype. In the usability test, we provides participants three scenarios and ask them to do a role play with the prototype and share their thoughts with us. With the results/ feedback of our usability test, the product function is redefined.

The design and requirements are documented in design specification.

Standardized Usability Questionnaires Analysis

Introduction

Survey is a universal and simple way of collecting huge amount of data in relatively short amount of time. However, according to my experience, conducting an effective survey is even more difficult than other user research technique. Researchers have to be really careful about all the words put on, structure, order and lots of other details.

Recently I have been reading research papers about standardized questionnaires and the book, quantifying user experience. Here are a summary of some useful notes and thoughts I got from the readings.

Advantage of applying standardized survey design
  • Objectivity
  • Replicability
  • Quantification
  • Economy
  • Communication
  • Scientific generalization
SUS
  • Fee:0 USD
  • 10 questions
  • Five point scale (Strongly disagree to strongly agree
  • Handy benchmarks
  • 12 samples do well on representing the whole data set
  • Global Reliability= 0.92
QUIS
  • Fee: 50-750 USD
  • 28 questions
  • Ten point scale (Terrible to Wonderful)
  • Global Reliability= 0.94
CSUQ (A variant of PSSUQ)
  • Fee: 0 USD
  • 19 Qeustions
  • Seven pomt scale(Strongly agree to disagree)
  • 12 samples can represent about 90% datasets
  • Global Reliability= 0.94
Takeaway

There are couple factors which need to pay attention to before making decision between survey methodology.

  • Potential Response Rate/ Sample Size
  • Budget
  • Degree of detail that you wish to learn from the result
  • Benchmark is requried or not
  • Type of product
  • Expectation to the survey result
Reference
  • 1. Thomas S. Tullis and Jacqueline N. Stetson (2004). A Comparison of Questionnaires for Assessing Website Usability. Retrieved from http://home.comcast.net/~tomtullis/publications/UPA2004TullisStetson.pdf
  • 2. Sauro, J., & Lewis, J. R. (2012). Quantifying the user experience: Practical statistics for user research. Amsterdam: Elsevier/Morgan Kaufmann.
